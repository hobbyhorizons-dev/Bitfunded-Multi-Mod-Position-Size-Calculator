What Is This Tool?

This is a community-built position size calculator designed for Bitfunded prop challenge traders. Its purpose is to reduce the gap between what a standard calculator predicts your loss will be, and what your account balance actually drops by when a stop loss market order triggers.

Standard position size calculators are mathematically correct for the price-movement portion of a loss. But they ignore the real-world costs added on top: platform fees, spread, and most significantly — slippage on stop loss market orders. Over time this unplanned overhead quietly erodes challenge performance in ways traders do not expect.

💡
This calculator adjusts position size downward so that after estimated costs are deducted, your actual account deduction still matches your planned risk amount. The adjustment is an estimate — not a guaranteed exact figure.
How to open and use the tool
  • The tool is a single HTML file — open by double-clicking in any browser
  • No installation, no account, no internet required after you have the file
  • Layout: inputs on the left, results on the right — no scrolling needed during use
  • Select a mode from the three tabs, fill the left panel, press Calculate or Enter

How the 14.33% Overhead Was Found

The 14.33% figure is not guessed. It was reverse-engineered from a single real trade on a real Bitfunded account.

The calibration trade
ParameterValue
AssetETH/USDT perpetual futures (long)
Position size$278,162
Entry price$2,347.42
Stop loss price$2,334.85
SL distance0.5355% from entry
Stop loss typeMarket order
Raw SL loss (math)$1,678.42 (position × SL%)
Actual balance drop$1,919.00
Overhead difference$240.58
Two equivalent ratios
Ratio A — % of raw SL loss (the 14.33%): $240.58 ÷ $1,678.42 = 14.33% Est. Total Loss = Raw SL Loss × 1.1433 Ratio B — % of position size (the 0.0865%): $240.58 ÷ $278,162 = 0.0865% Overhead = Position Size × 0.0865%

Both ratios describe the same $240.58 overhead — two views of the same data. The calculator uses Ratio B internally because it makes back-calculation of adjusted position sizes cleaner.

📌
Why this is an estimate, not a guarantee: Derived from one trade on one asset at one position size. Different assets, sizes, and market conditions will produce different overhead. Use as a precautionary planning factor — it is far more accurate than ignoring costs entirely.
Market Stop Orders & Slippage

When a stop loss is set as a market order, the platform sends a market order the moment the trigger price is reached. That order fills at whatever price the market is offering at that millisecond — not the stop price you set. The difference is slippage.

In calm conditions slippage is small. During fast-moving markets — precisely when stop losses are most likely to trigger — the market can gap past your level and fill significantly worse. The $240.58 overhead in the calibration trade included this slippage effect alongside platform commissions.

Why market orders were used in the calibration trade

The calibration trade used a market order for the stop loss — this is the most common choice for prop traders because it guarantees execution. The slippage observed is therefore realistic and representative of what traders experience.

⚠
Limit stop order trade-off: A limit stop only fills at your exact price — no slippage. But if the market gaps through your level during news events or flash crashes, the order may not fill at all. A stop that fails to fill means your position runs unchecked — if it reaches the maximum daily loss or total loss threshold unfilled, the challenge can be breached with no ability to act. Market stops trade a predictable small slippage cost for guaranteed fill. Most professional prop traders accept this deliberately.
The Adjusted Position Size Formula
STANDARD (ignores overhead): Position = Max Loss ÷ SL% ESTIMATION-ADJUSTED (this tool): Goal: Raw SL Loss + Overhead = Max Planned Loss Position × SL% + Position × 0.0865% = Max Loss Position × (SL% + 0.0865%) = Max Loss Adjusted Position = Max Loss ÷ (SL% + 0.0865%) Where: Max Loss = Balance × Risk% Required Margin = Adjusted Position ÷ Leverage Overhead Est. = Adjusted Position × 0.0865% Est. Total Loss = (Position × SL%) + Overhead ≈ Max Loss ✓
Worked example
InputValue
Balance$150,000
Risk %0.25%
Leverage5×
SL distance0.54%
Max Planned Loss = $150,000 × 0.25% = $375.00 Adj. Position = $375.00 ÷ (0.54% + 0.0865%) = $59,857 Required Margin = $59,857 ÷ 5 = $11,971 Raw SL Loss = $59,857 × 0.54% = $323.23 Overhead Estimate = $59,857 × 0.0865% = $51.77 Est. Total Loss = $323.23 + $51.77 = $375.00 ✓
✅
Enter $59,857 as position size. If SL triggers, account drops ~$375 — matching planned risk exactly.

Risk Level Scale — Challenge Context

Standard tools label 1% risk as conservative. That does not apply to prop firm challenges with defined maximum loss limits. This tool uses a scale calibrated for challenge account sizes.

≤ 0.25%ConservativeRecommended. Large buffer before any limit.
0.26%–0.30%FlexibleStill within safe operating range.
0.31%–0.50%AverageWorkable but reduces your SL budget.
0.51%–1.00%AggressiveIncreases risk of hitting limits on bad streaks.
Above 1.00%Extreme RiskA few consecutive losses may breach challenge rules.

The panel also calculates how many consecutive SL hits at your chosen risk level would breach each limit — giving a practical sense of your buffer.

Challenge Maximum Loss Floors

Bitfunded measures maximum loss from the initial starting balance of a brand-new account at each tier — not from the current balance. The floor is permanently set on day one and does NOT rise with profits.

⚠
Common mistake: assuming the max loss limit rises as your balance grows. It does not. A $150,000 tier account always has its floor at $138,000 — even if the balance grows to $160,000.

Notes & Remarks
📌
This section is reserved for additional observations, known limitations, and community-reported notes gathered through ongoing testing. It will be updated as new information is confirmed.
Known Limitations
  • The 0.0865% overhead factor was derived from a single calibration trade on ETH/USDT perpetuals. Other assets, different session times, and varying position sizes may produce different overhead.
  • This tool does not track, display, or calculate the Bitfunded Daily Maximum Loss limit. Always monitor daily drawdown independently on the Bitfunded dashboard.
  • Tier detection uses midpoint cutoffs between standard Bitfunded account sizes. If your balance is near a midpoint boundary, verify your actual tier manually.
  • Slippage estimates will differ in low-liquidity conditions, major news events, or during market gaps. The factor is a planning reference — not a real-time cost guarantee.
General Remarks
  • Always cross-reference calculated position sizes with your own risk management rules before entering a trade.
  • The tool is designed for Bitfunded futures with a maximum 5× leverage cap. Using it for other platforms with different leverage structures will require adaptation.
  • Results are estimates. Treat them as a planning aid, not as an exact execution guarantee.
